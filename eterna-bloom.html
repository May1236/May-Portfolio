Eterna Bloom

A boutique floral studio built on one idea — “handcrafted florals that celebrate love, beauty and meaningful moments.” Calm, natural, effortlessly elegant.

Market

Overview

A florist worth remembering.

Eterna Bloom is a premium florist focused on meaningful gifting experiences. The goal was a complete visual identity plus a small storytelling campaign that could carry the brand consistently across digital and in-store touchpoints, aimed at thoughtful gift-buyers aged 20–40 in Calgary.

The problem

Standing out in a crowded market.

Calgary's floral market is highly competitive, and customers struggle to choose meaningful gifts while wanting elegant, reliable options. As a new brand, Eterna Bloom had no recognizable identity — and without a cohesive visual system, it risked looking generic and untrustworthy across the very touchpoints where first impressions are made.

Moodboard — calm, natural, effortlessly elegant.

Approach

Craft you can feel.

I designed a handcrafted identity — a minimalist rose mark paired with elegant handwritten typography — so the logo itself signals the care behind every arrangement. Terracotta Red and Leaf Green evoke flowers and nature, while a Champagne accent with Playfair Display + Myriad Pro keeps everything premium and warm.

From there I built an emotional mini-campaign: because people buy flowers to express feeling, not décor, the storytelling leads with emotion and the system reinforces professionalism across web, social, packaging and signage.

Results & impact

A system that sells the feeling.

The project delivered a complete 17-page brand guideline and a 20-slide pitch deck, approved across two rounds of refinement, with one unified look across print and digital. The campaign targeted a 30% lift in brand awareness and a 6–8% social engagement rate.

Beyond the numbers, the identity gives Eterna Bloom stronger recognition, a premium perception, and a clear point of difference in a generic market — plus a reusable foundation that makes all future marketing faster and more consistent.

Reflection

What it taught me.

This project sharpened my ability to translate emotional brand values into a cohesive visual system — and reminded me that consistency across every touchpoint is what actually builds trust.
